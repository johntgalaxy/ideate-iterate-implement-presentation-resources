Interaction Design Guide

Choose the form.
Name the role.

An interaction has both a form and an instructional role. A simulation is a species; its purpose depends on placement and design.

01

Interaction forms or species

Choose the form that makes the target learner action possible.

Simulation

Change a condition and observe a modeled consequence.

Case Study

Inspect evidence and justify a conclusion.

Scenario

Make a decision and examine consequences.

Narrative

Follow or influence a sequence of events.

Visual Stimulus

Notice, interpret, or respond to a visual source.

Interactive Diagram

Reveal, connect, compare, or manipulate labeled parts.

Timeline

Sequence events and inspect change over time.

Decision Tree

Follow choices, criteria, and branching outcomes.

Gamified Activity

Practice through bounded goals, feedback, and replay.

Practice Exercise

Rehearse a skill with explanation-based feedback.

Guided Conversation

Respond to prompts that scaffold reasoning.

Chat Assistant

Ask bounded questions within verified content.

Reflection

Articulate reasoning, confidence, or a next step.

Data Visualization

Filter, compare, or interpret a data pattern.

Presentation Artifact

Explore a structured explanation or demonstration.

Interest Artifact

Build curiosity before deeper instruction.

Design principle

Form and role are related, but they are not interchangeable.

The same simulation might present a concept, provide deliberate practice, support formative assessment, or prepare transfer.

02

Instructional roles

What job does the artifact perform in the larger learning experience?

  • Orientation
  • Concept presentation
  • Interest or curiosity
  • Deliberate practice
  • Formative assessment
  • Reflection
  • Application
  • Transfer
  • Summative assessment
  • Reference or support
03

Complete the interaction decision

Form
What kind of artifact is it?
Role
Why does it exist at this course moment?
Student action
What will learners change, choose, compare, explain, interpret, or produce?
Response
What consequence, feedback, explanation, or status appears?
Progression
Is it exploratory, guided, branching, staged, or replayable?
Boundary
What is the smallest convincing slice you can test?
04

Prompt pattern

Use a [form] as [instructional role]. Learners will [specific action].
When they act, [visible consequence, feedback, explanation, or status] appears.
Use [guided/exploratory/branching/staged] progression and provide [reset, retry, replay, or transfer move].
Keep the first build to [smallest convincing slice].

Cross-discipline reminder

Start with the learning verb, not the discipline label.

These are affinities, not boundaries. Any discipline may use comparison, narrative, simulation, scenario, reflection, or deliberate practice.
