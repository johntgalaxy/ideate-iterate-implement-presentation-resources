Iteration Checklist

Improve one dimension at a time

Do not ask AI to “make it better.” Diagnose the most important mismatch, request one targeted change, preserve what works, and test the result.

01

The seven-step loop

The order of the lenses is flexible; the discipline of the loop is not.

  1. Generate or revise
  2. Review through one lens
  3. Name the most important mismatch
  4. Request a targeted change
  5. State what must remain unchanged
  6. Test the result
  7. Continue and repeat as necessary

Lens 01

Learning Context

Ask: Does this serve the intended learning, learners, and course moment?

  • Outcome and assessment alignment
  • Course and module placement
  • Learner readiness and prerequisites
  • Purpose before and after the activity
  • Appropriate difficulty, scope, and duration
  • Value relative to the surrounding lesson
Revise the activity so students compare the two models rather than merely identify their parts. Preserve the existing visual layout and controls.

Lens 02

Interaction Design

Ask: What does the learner do, and how does the artifact respond?

  • Meaningful learner action
  • Choice, change, comparison, or explanation
  • Consequences and useful feedback
  • Progression rather than repetition
  • Useful challenge and cognitive effort
  • Relationship between form and role
  • Contribution to the synthetic object
Add one branching decision with a visible consequence and concise feedback. Preserve the current scenario, tone, and visual style.

Targeted revision

Name the mismatch, the change, and what must remain unchanged.

A focused request makes the design decision visible and limits collateral changes.

Continue the review

Trustworthy content. Testable artifact.

Accessibility, visual design, usability, and technical quality belong within Artifact Specification—not in a fifth lens.

Lens 03

Expert Content

Ask: Are the claims, examples, models, and explanations trustworthy?

  • Accuracy and source authority
  • Disciplinary language and conventions
  • Appropriate simplification
  • Examples, nonexamples, and edge cases
  • Assumptions and limitations
  • Tacit expert reasoning made visible
  • Consistency across labels, data, and feedback
Correct the explanation of orbital eccentricity using the supplied notes. Preserve the calculations, controls, and layout. Name the model’s simplifying assumption.

Lens 04

Artifact Specification

Ask: Does it work visually, accessibly, technically, and in delivery?

  • Visual clarity and hierarchy
  • Keyboard and assistive-technology access
  • Labels, focus, equivalent text, and status
  • Responsive and screen-size behavior
  • Navigation, reset, replay, and completion
  • Files, paths, packaging, iframe, and SCORM
  • Performance, rights, privacy, captions, and credits
Make every control keyboard operable and expose visual state as status text. Preserve the calculations, sequence, and visual composition.

Guiding principle

Ideate → Iterate → Implement

The loop ends when the artifact is fit for its instructional purpose and delivery context—not merely when AI produces another version.
