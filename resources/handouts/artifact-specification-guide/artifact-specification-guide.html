Artifact Specification Guide

Make the artifact
testable

Define what the finished artifact must look like, do, support, and comply with. Accessibility, aesthetics, usability, testing, and quality assurance belong here—not in a fifth category.

01

Specify the deliverable

Describe the behavior and quality that implementation must produce.

Deliverable + behavior

  • Artifact type, format, duration, and scope
  • Tone, visual style, branding, palette, and type
  • Controls, instructions, navigation, and feedback
  • Guided, exploratory, branching, or staged behavior
  • Reset, retry, replay, progress, and completion

Access + visual quality

  • Keyboard controls and logical focus order
  • Labels, equivalent text, and announced status
  • Contrast, motion, captions, and color-independent meaning
  • Responsive behavior and required screen sizes
  • Hierarchy, readability, spacing, usability, and polish

Delivery + constraints

  • Canvas placement, iframe, link, or launch
  • SCORM version and completion behavior
  • Files, paths, editable source, and deployable package
  • Local, hosted, generated, or licensed assets
  • Rights, privacy, integrations, dependencies, and prohibitions
02

Write the specification

Create a [length] [artifact type] in [technical format] for [delivery environment].
Use [visual direction, tone, and brand requirements].
Learners will use [controls and navigation] and receive [feedback/status].
Support [keyboard, equivalent text, responsive behavior, captions, or other access requirements].
Include [reset, progress, and completion behavior].
Organize files as [source/package requirements] and use [asset rules].
Do not use [prohibited features, dependencies, accounts, or data practices].
Test [required behaviors, screens, packaging, and delivery conditions].
