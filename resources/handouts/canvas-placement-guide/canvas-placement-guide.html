Instructional Placement Guide

Place the artifact
intentionally

Implementation does not end when the interactive works. Design the surrounding instructions, reflection, assessment, and other components that make the artifact a purposeful part of the learning experience.

01

Choose the instructional placement

The interactive is one component of a larger synthetic object.

Learning useInstructional placementBest when...Follow with...
Concept introductionBefore explanation or demonstrationStudents need a first encounter with a process or modelA short reflection or explanation
Guided practiceAfter modeling, before independent workStudents need low-stakes rehearsalPractice questions, coaching, or a knowledge check
Lab or performance preparationBefore a procedure, field task, or demonstrationStudents need to preview a system or sequenceLab task, worksheet, or check-in
Review activityBefore retrieval, synthesis, or assessmentStudents need to revisit a conceptStudy guide, discussion, or assessment
Optional enrichmentAlongside required work, clearly labeledStudents can explore beyond the required pathA self-directed extension or resource
02

Answer the placement questions

Before and during

  1. What learning problem does it address?
  2. What comes immediately before it?
  3. How is its purpose introduced?
  4. What action, observation, decision, or explanation does it prepare?

After and around

  1. What should learners do afterward?
  2. How will they understand what they completed or learned?
  3. Is the artifact central or supporting?
  4. What surrounding material makes the experience cohere?
03

Design the connective tissue

Before

Introduce the learner’s purpose and give concise instructions at the point of need.

After

Follow with reflection, feedback, practice, discussion, or assessment that uses what learners just did.

04

Write the instructional placement decision

Use this [length and format] activity after [preceding learning experience] and before [next learning step].
Introduce it by telling learners [purpose and why it matters now].
Learners will [action or thinking task]; afterward, they will [reflection, practice, discussion, assessment, or transfer task].
The artifact is [central/supporting] within the larger synthetic object.

Don't mistake delivery for instruction!

Canvas serves as our a delivery host, and it's not to be confused with the placement decision.

First determine where the artifact is needed in the instructional sequence; then configure the platform to support that sequence.
