Learning Context Guide

What situation is this
artifact designed to serve?

Begin with outcomes and assessment when possible. Make the learners, purpose, placement, prerequisites, difficulty, time, and surrounding course sequence visible before and during the first build.

01

Match the learning moment to the learning use

Use the course flow to decide what students should do before and after an activity.

Learning usePlace it in the course as...Best when...Follow with...
Concept introductionA lesson or learning pageStudents need a first encounter with a process or modelA short reflection prompt
Practice before a knowledge checkA short practice step in the course sequenceStudents need low-stakes rehearsalPractice questions or a knowledge check
Lab preparationDirections or a preparation stepStudents need to preview a procedure or systemLab task, worksheet, or check-in
Review activityA review stepStudents need to revisit a concept before assessmentStudy guide or discussion
Optional enrichmentAn optional explorationStudents can explore beyond the required pathA clear optional label
02

Context questions

Record what you know; let missing information become part of the AI conversation.

  1. Which course, unit, and module?
  2. Which outcome and related assessment?
  3. Who are the learners?
  4. What prerequisite knowledge can you assume?
  5. What is the learning purpose and instructional role?
  6. What comes immediately before and after?
  7. How much time and difficulty are appropriate?
  8. Is the artifact central, supporting, optional, exploratory, reflective, or evaluative?
03

Student-facing framing

Use one or two plain sentences near the activity.

Use this short practice activity to observe how ______ changes when ______.
Afterward, answer the question below.
This activity is for practice, not grading. Try it more than once and focus on what changes between attempts.

Backward-design move

Outcome → assessment evidence → interaction.

The interactive is one component of a larger synthetic object, not necessarily the whole lesson.
